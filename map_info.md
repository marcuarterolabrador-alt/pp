


#### Marine and Freshwater Research Centre from ATU (https://mfrc-atu.ie/)

The Marine and Freshwater Research Centre 
(MFRC) is a research institute established in 2009 and based at the Atlantic Technological 
University (ATU) Galway Campus on the west coast of Ireland. The MFRC focuses on 
conducting applied research, joining environmental conservation and societal needs, 
working mostly on the Irish coast. It is primarily focused on marine and aquatic systems, with 
research groups that range from marine mammal, genetics, fisheries, to microplastics. The 
centre includes a wide range of scientific areas, hosting also some terrestrial ecology 
research groups (Marine and Freshwater Research Centre, 2026).  
The core operational philosophy of the MFRC is to produce policy-relevant, evidence-based 
data. To achieve this, the centre actively partners with public sector stakeholders, state 
agencies, and commercial industries to create practical management solutions for natural 
resources. Its research is driven by the following three main pillars or goals: - - - 
Enabling sustainability: Collecting and interpreting ecological data to ensure the 
long-term, sustainable management of commercial fisheries and marine ecosystems. 
Conserving biodiversity: Assessing how aquatic species and habitats respond to 
accelerating human impacts and climate-induced environmental stressors. 
Improving productivity: Working alongside the seafood sector to identify and 
implement innovative technologies that optimise production while safeguarding 
ecological integrity. 


Aligning directly with the MFRC’s core pillars of enabling sustainability and working 
alongside industry stakeholders, the Native Oyster restoration initiatives are carried out in close collaboration with 
fishermen from the Galway & Aran Fishermen's Co-operative. The research group hires local 
fishermen and their vessels for fieldwork, directly integrating their historical knowledge and 
experience into their research and fostering community engagement, sensitisation, and 
co-management.
